## Don't rinse off the minerals
After your soak, it is generally best not to wash off straight away, so your skin keeps the beneficial minerals from the spring. A light cold rinse is a healthy alternative some people enjoy and has its own invigorating benefits. The exception is sensitive skin, or strongly acidic or sulfur springs, where rinsing with fresh water afterward is recommended to avoid irritation.

## Rest and rehydrate
Get out of the bath slowly to avoid dizziness, then dress and rest quietly for around 30 minutes, as your blood pressure settles and your body has used energy. Many onsen have rest areas, lounges, and massage chairs for exactly this. Rehydrate with water, tea, or a sports drink, and take care not to get chilled. Avoid alcohol afterward, as it dehydrates you further.

## Post-bath treats
A cold drink after a hot bath is a classic part of the experience. Popular choices are furutsu gyuunyuu (fruit milk) or koohii gyuunyuu (coffee milk), often sold at the onsen. Many hot-spring areas also serve onsen tamago — eggs slow-cooked in the hot-spring water and steam — and some onsen sell bottled, mineral-rich spring water to take away.
