## Step 1 — Undress in the changing room
Enter the correct changing room — red noren curtains usually mark the women's bath and blue the men's, with 女 for women and 男 for men. Remove all your clothing and place it in a basket or locker. Take only your small towel into the bathing area.

## Step 2 — Rinse before entering (kakeyu)
Before getting into the bath, rinse your body. Sit at a washing station and use the shower, or scoop water from the bath with a bucket and pour it over yourself, starting from your feet and working up. This both cleans you and lets your body adjust to the hot water.

## Step 3 — Wash your body
Sit on the stool at the washing station and wash thoroughly with soap and shampoo. Rinse off all suds completely so no soap reaches the communal bath. Keep the water and bubbles contained at your station out of consideration for others.

## Step 4 — Soak slowly
Enter the bath gently and quietly without splashing. Keep your small towel out of the water, resting it on your head or the side. The water is hot, often 40–43°C, so ease in and soak calmly. Do not put your head under or swim.

## Step 5 — Rest between soaks
If you feel hot or light-headed, step out and sit on the edge or cool off for a few minutes, then return. Alternating short soaks with rest is normal and comfortable. Listen to your body and do not overdo the heat.

## Step 6 — Finish and dry off
When you are done, you can pat off excess water with your small towel before returning to the changing room — this keeps the floor dry. Whether to rinse off the mineral water afterward is personal preference; some leave it on to enjoy the spring's effects. Dress, drink water, and rest. Many people enjoy a cold drink after bathing.
