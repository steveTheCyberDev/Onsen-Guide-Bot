## Onsen through the seasons
Onsen are enjoyed year-round, and each season offers something different. The contrast between the hot water and the outside air is a big part of the appeal, which is why many travellers consider the cooler months the classic onsen season. That said, a good onsen is rewarding in any month.

## Winter — the classic season
Winter is the most popular time for onsen. Soaking in an outdoor bath (rotenburo) while surrounded by cold air or falling snow — a scene known as yukimi-buro, the "snow-viewing bath" — is a signature Japanese experience. The heat feels especially good against the cold, and snowy mountain regions are prized for it. Dress warmly for the walk between your room and the bath.

## Spring and autumn
Spring and autumn are comfortable, scenic times to visit. In spring, some onsen towns have cherry blossoms; in autumn, mountain areas turn red and gold with foliage (koyo), and an outdoor bath framed by autumn leaves is a highlight. Mild temperatures make these shoulder seasons pleasant for combining bathing with walking and sightseeing.

## Summer
Summer onsen visits are still enjoyable, especially in cooler highland and northern regions where the air is fresh. Early morning or evening soaks avoid the midday heat, and rotenburo with mountain breezes are refreshing. Stay well hydrated, as the combination of hot baths and hot weather can tire you quickly. Higher-altitude onsen areas make a good summer escape from the city heat.

## Regional notes
Northern and mountainous areas (such as Hokkaido and the Japan Alps) are especially associated with snow-season bathing. Warmer southern regions, including Okinawa, have a milder climate and are comfortable for much of the year. When planning, consider both the onsen itself and the surrounding scenery, which changes dramatically with the season.
