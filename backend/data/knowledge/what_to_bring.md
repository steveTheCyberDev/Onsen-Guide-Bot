## Towels
Bring two towels: a small "wash" towel to take into the bathing area for modesty and patting off water, and a larger drying towel for the changing room. At a ryokan, a hand (wash) towel and a drying towel are typically provided in your room. At day-use onsen you can usually bring your own, and many provide, rent, or sell a towel cheaply at reception, so you do not always need to pack one.

**Source:** JAL — https://www.jal.co.jp/cn/en/guide-to-japan/plan-your-trip/tips/things-to-know-before-onsen-visit.html

## Toiletries
Most onsen supply soap, body wash, and shampoo at the washing stations, and ryokan usually provide them too; if a facility does not, you may need to buy some on-site. If you are particular about your products, or visiting a very simple local bath, bring your own travel-size soap and shampoo. Any skincare you like to use after bathing is a useful addition.

**Source:** JAL — https://www.jal.co.jp/cn/en/guide-to-japan/plan-your-trip/tips/things-to-know-before-onsen-visit.html

## Practical items
Bring a hair tie if you have long hair, since hair must be kept out of the water. Carry cash, as smaller onsen and lockers may not accept cards. A small bag for wet items and your own water bottle to rehydrate after the heat are both handy. If you wear glasses, note they can fog and the floors are slippery. You usually do not need to pack a comb or hairdryer — changing rooms generally have these and similar amenities free to use.

**Source:** JAL — https://www.jal.co.jp/cn/en/guide-to-japan/plan-your-trip/tips/things-to-know-before-onsen-visit.html

## What you do not need
You do not need swimwear — onsen are enjoyed nude and swimwear is not allowed in the bathing area. You also do not need to bring your own towels if your accommodation provides them; check in advance. A ryokan provides a yukata (a light cotton robe) to wear after bathing and to relax in, so you need not pack sleepwear. Leave valuables out of the changing-room basket — store them in a locker or at the front desk rather than carrying them to the bath.
