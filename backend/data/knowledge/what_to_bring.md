## Towels
Bring two towels: a small "wash" towel to take into the bathing area for modesty and patting off water, and a larger bath towel to dry yourself in the changing room. Many ryokan and some day-use onsen provide both, and others rent or sell a small towel cheaply at reception, so you do not always need to pack your own.

## Toiletries
Most onsen supply soap, body wash, and shampoo at the washing stations, and ryokan usually provide them too. If you are particular about your products, or visiting a very simple local bath, bring your own travel-size soap and shampoo. A comb or hairbrush and any skincare you like to use after bathing are useful additions.

## Practical items
Bring a hair tie if you have long hair, since hair must be kept out of the water. Carry cash, as smaller onsen and lockers may not accept cards. A small bag for wet items and your own water bottle to rehydrate after the heat are both handy. If you wear glasses, note they can fog and the floors are slippery.

## What you do not need
You do not need swimwear — onsen are enjoyed nude and swimwear is not allowed in the bathing area. You also do not need to bring your own bath towel if your accommodation provides one; check in advance. Leave valuables in a locker or at the front desk rather than carrying them to the bath.
